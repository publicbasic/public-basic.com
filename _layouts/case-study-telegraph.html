Like many legacy businesses around the world, the Telegraph is in the middle a radical digital transformation. We were engaged to support a newly established Product and UX function by developing an in-house optimisation program.



What we did

During the course of our engagement with the Telegraph we:

  • Worked between the Telegraph's IT, procurement and legal teams to find an implement a suitable A/B testing platform
  • Defined roles, processes and governance for test development, QA, release and results distribution
  • Implemented a method for prioritising the experiment backlog
  • Worked with stakeholders across the business to design experiments to give useful data quickly
  • Set-up a framework for test planning and reporting documentation and distribution.

The results

Over the course of a 18-month engagement we enabled the business to become self sufficient and build up a robust in-house A/B testing capacity in-house.

We also achieved some pretty great results from our tests:

  • 8% increase in subscription sales
  • 14% lift in video plays
  • 22% increase in user engagement

We championed A/B tested and helped to show demonstrable value in the power of optimisation, so that now any new feature or tweak invariably begins life validated on a small portion of traffic as an A/B test variant.
