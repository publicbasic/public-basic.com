Client

Telegraph Media Group
Services

A/B Testing

Interface design



Telegraph.co.uk was the very first newspaper in Europe to establish a daily edition, launching in November in 1994 from a Sun Sparc 20 on a leased 64/kbit line from their Canary Wharf newsroom.

Over 20 years later, the site receives 70m+ visits per month in addition to its suite of phone and tablet apps.

The business is in the midst of a large scale digital transformation, moving toward agile methodology, short sprint cycles and the establishment of a data team of analysts, data scientists and user researchers.



What we did

Working closely with the business for over a year, we advised on the establishment of an in-house A/B testing program.

  • Helped to find a suitable A/B testing platform
  • Prioritised a back-log of experiments
  • Worked with stakeholders across the business to design experiments to give useful data quickly
  • Set-up a framework for test planning and reporting documentation and distribution.

The results

Over the course of a 18-month engagement we enabled the business to become self sufficient and build up a robust in-house A/B testing capacity in-house.

We worked across the UX, product and data science teams to help to embed a culture of testing and optimisation with the business where any new initiative on the site typically begins with a test.
